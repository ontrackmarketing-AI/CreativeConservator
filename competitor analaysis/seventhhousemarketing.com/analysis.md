# Seventh House Marketing - Visual & Branding Analysis

## Overview
- **Website:** seventhhousemarketing.com
- **Tagline:** "We're creatives at heart driven by purposeful strategy"
- **Platform:** Squarespace

---

## Color Palette

| Color | Hex (Estimated) | Usage |
|-------|-----------------|-------|
| Sage Green | #889063 | Navigation bar, footer, accent sections |
| Soft Pink/Blush | #F5C6C6 | Large section backgrounds |
| Cream/Off-White | #F8F5F0 | Primary backgrounds |
| Forest Green | #4A5D3C | Text, headings |
| Terracotta/Coral | #D4826A | Accent elements |
| White | #FFFFFF | Cards, overlays |

**Palette Style:** Fresh, feminine, modern yet sophisticated. Pink and green combination feels trendy and approachable. Aligns well with lifestyle/beauty brands.

---

## Typography

| Element | Style |
|---------|-------|
| H1 Headlines | Elegant serif with italic emphasis on key words |
| H2/H3 Subheadings | Serif, refined |
| Body Text | Clean sans-serif |
| CTAs | Sans-serif, lowercase/sentence case |

**Typography Style:** Sophisticated serif headlines paired with readable sans-serif body. Italic emphasis creates movement and interest.

## Strengths to Consider
- Strong brand positioning ("Millennial & Gen Z Whisperers")
- Impressive client logos and testimonials
- Media features add credibility
- Multiple revenue streams (services + education)
- Clear service categorization
- Fresh, on-trend aesthetic

## Weaknesses to Avoid
- Pink/green palette may feel too trendy/dated quickly
- "Steal our strategies" could feel gimmicky
- Some sections feel visually busy

---

## Relevance to Creative Conservator

- **Color alignment:** Their sage green (#889063) matches Darya's Green perfectly
- **Typography approach:** Serif with italic emphasis is similar to target
- Could adopt: Service accordion, client testimonial carousel, media features bar
- Differentiate with: Deeper, more classical palette (wine/cream vs pink/green), more refined/elevated vs. trendy positioning, artistic imagery vs. lifestyle
- The educational/academy model is interesting for future expansion
